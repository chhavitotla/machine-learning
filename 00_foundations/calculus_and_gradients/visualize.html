Calculus & Gradients

The derivative is the slope under your feet; gradient descent is "feel the slope, step the other way, repeat." Drag the cyan point along the curve to read its tangent. Shrink h to watch the amber secant collapse onto the tangent. Then drop the ball and let it walk downhill — and crank α until it overshoots.

As h → 0 the secant slope (f(x+h) − f(x)) / h converges to the tangent slope. That limit is the derivative.
