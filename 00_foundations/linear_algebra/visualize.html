Matrices Transform Space

A matrix is a machine that warps the whole plane at once. Its columns are simply where the two unit arrows land. Set the four entries of M, then scrub or play the morph from identity to M — and watch the unit square's area report the determinant, and the amber eigenvector arrows mark the directions M refuses to rotate.
