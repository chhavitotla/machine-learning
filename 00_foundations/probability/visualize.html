Uncertainty, Quantified

Two pillars of probability, live. Top: drop balls that each take n coin-flip steps — chaos per ball, yet the pile is always the same bell curve (the Central Limit Theorem). Bottom: Bayes' rule on a population of 10,000 — watch a "99% accurate" test produce mostly false alarms when the disease is rare.
