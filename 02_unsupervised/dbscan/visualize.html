DBSCAN

No K to choose, no centers to drop. A cluster is wherever points are dense: core points (≥ min_samples neighbors within ε) chain to other core points, and color floods outward through the chain. Move your mouse over the canvas to feel the ε radius, load a shape k-means can't handle, and hit Run.

bright ring = core · small dot = border · gray ✕ = noise · click to add points · circle under mouse = ε
ε too small → everything is noise. ε too big → the moons fuse into one blob. There is no ε that works when one cluster is dense and another sparse — DBSCAN's honest weakness.
