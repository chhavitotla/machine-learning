The Perceptron

The 1958 rule that started it all: visit points one at a time; if a point is misclassified, add α·y·x to the weights and the boundary visibly rotates toward it. On separable data this provably converges. Hit make non-separable and watch it oscillate forever — the exact failure that demanded loss functions and hidden layers.

white arrow: w · amber arrow: the update α·y·x being added · pulsing ring: current point
α only scales the step here — unlike gradient descent, the perceptron converges for any α on separable data, because each update is the same fixed correction, just resized.
