RNNs & LSTMs

A recurrent network is one cell applied over and over: feed in "hello world" a character at a time and the unrolled chain grows left to right, every hidden state genuinely computed from seeded weights. Then flip on gradient flow and watch the backward pass: the RNN's gradient shrinks by a factor < 1 at every step back — the LSTM's cell-state highway keeps it alive.
